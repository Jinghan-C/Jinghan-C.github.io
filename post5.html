The Obstacle to obtaining knowledge according to Montaigne and Bacon

By Jinghan Cao Nov 2019

The greatest obstacle that humans face in the search for knowledge, according to Montaigne and Bacon, is the desire for certainty. People are so conservative that they reject uncertainties. People tend to make judgments based on their own beliefs and opinions and this way of thinking restricts the knowledge people have. The desire for certainty becomes a hindrance to humans’ search of knowledge because it can lead to a life of denial and ignorance, it lowers people’s awareness for false knowledge and it makes their ideas antiquated. By discussing the problem of desiring certainties, Montaigne and Bacon both bring up the idea of skepticism which is a new method of assessing the nature of knowledge.

Montaigne and Bacon both agree that the desire for certainty is a major obstacle that humans face in the search for knowledge because it leads to a life full of denial and ignorance. People desire certainty because they are used to staying in their comfort zone and having everything under control. They deny anything they are uncertain about. In To Philosophize is to learn how to die, Montaigne discusses that people should be ready to face death instead of worrying about it all the time. However, for most people “the remedy is not to think about it; but what brutish insensitivity can produce so gross a blindness?” (Montaigne, To Philosophize is to learn how to die, 20). Montaigne calls those who are afraid to face death as blindness. People avoid thinking about death because their knowledge about death is uncertain. They are afraid of death because they can’t control it. But ignoring death doesn’t solve the problem. The rejection of learning death due to its uncertainty only makes people more ignorant and causes more problems. Instead of avoiding the problem, Montaigne argues that people should face it and fight against it: “let us deprive death of its strangeness; let us frequent it, let us get used to it; let us have nothing more often in mind than death.”(Montaigne,24). People should face and accept uncertainty rather than avoiding it. It is the acceptance that solves the problem, not denial. Similarly, Bacon argues the same idea in New Organon. He talks about the problem of how people understand knowledge. People tend to believe in what they are certain about and what they considered as right. For those contrary ideas, they “either neglect and despise, or else by some distinction sets aside and rejects, in order that by this great and pernicious predetermination the authority of its former conclusion may remain inviolate”(Bacon, New Organon, 46). Once people adopt an idea they are certain about, they tend to deny everything that is opposed to it. They don’t want to be proved wrong nor to be challenged by the knowledge they have. They reject other information which “indeed, in the establishment of any true axiom, the negative instance is more forcible of the two”(Bacon, 46). Bacon argues that the desire for certainty limits people’s thoughts. By not being skeptical, people may miss the truth. The desire for certainty and rejection of other ideas makes people ignorant. Bacon and Montaigne both discuss the problem of people expecting certainty. The knowledge they have is limited by their desire for certainty. They both agree that it can cause denial and ignorance in people’s lives, and becomes an obstacle for people to learn knowledge.

The desire for certainty also lowers people’s awareness of false knowledge, which can be an important hindrance in people’s search for knowledge. People desire certainty, so they tend to agree on something that matches their beliefs without testing it, and deny any new knowledge that they are uncertain about. It is likely for people to get false knowledge when they reject anything new and not being skeptical about their knowledge. In the Essays, Montaigne brings up the example of a guy’s first time seeing a river: “the first time he did so took it for the ocean, since we think that the biggest things that we know represent the limits of what Nature can produce in that species.”(Montaigne, That it is madness to judge the true and the false from our own capacities,75). The guy is making a judgment based on his limited knowledge and the conclusion he comes up with is obviously wrong. Montaigne argues that people are likely to come up with false conclusions because they don’t test their knowledge. People are not skeptical because it leads to uncertainty. Once they start questioning, there will be uncertainties. So people prefer to just be satisfied with the knowledge they have, even if the information they have is wrong. Reject uncertainties lowers their awareness of false knowledge. Montaigne suggested that “if we cannot be convinced we should at least remain in suspense”(Montaigne, 76) He thinks that if people cannot agree with the contrary side, they should at least be questioning the knowledge. Bacon, agreeing with Montaigne, argues for the same idea: “Read not to contradict and confute, nor to believe and take for granted, nor to find talk and discourse, but to weigh and consider”(Bacon, of studies) Bacon suggested that accepting uncertainties is not to confute any idea, but to test and reconsider their idea. People should be testing and reflecting since it is human nature to have false judgment: “The Idols of the Tribe have their foundation in human nature itself, and in the tribe or race of men. For it is a false assertion that the sense of man is the measure of things”(Bacon, New Organon, 41) Bacon thinks that people tend to judge based on their own knowledge. It is something humans are born with and it can lead to some false ideas or knowledge. Humans should test their knowledge and seek the truth because this false assertion can lead to false information. It increases the possibility for people to learn false knowledge when they aren't questioning. In Montaigne and Bacon’s work, the idea of skepticism is discussed in contrast to humans’ desire for certainty. Both of them present evidence that the result of rejecting skepticism is biased judgments and less awareness of false knowledge. They conclude that people’s desire for certainty is a major obstacle on the path of seeking knowledge because it lowers people’s awareness of false knowledge.

Besides, both Montaigne and Bacon agree that humans’ desire for certainty can be a hindrance to the search of knowledge for humans because it keeps their minds antiquated. According to Montaigne and Bacon, people prefer conventions over innovations. It is easy for humans to feel satisfied and it keeps them from discovering new knowledge. People like to live in a world where everything is certain and they don’t have to question anything. However, it makes them ignorant and makes them lag behind reality. Montaigne demonstrating the idea that “If we do not keep them busy with some particular subject which can serve as a bridle to reign them in , they charge ungovernable about, ranging to and fro over the wastelands of our thoughts”(Montaigne, on idleness, 9) Montaigne uses himself as an example to argue that idleness is not something people should pursue. Instead, people should be constantly thinking and questioning, otherwise, they will get lost. People who desire certainty are like people who live in idleness. Their minds are left idle as Montaigne described. Reject uncertainties prevent people from discovering new information which ultimately separates people from social reality. Therefore, Montaigne implies that in order to keep up with social development, people have to be skeptical and test their knowledge. Bacon, by the same token, also presents evidence that the desire for certainty leads to antiquated ideas. Bacon uses medicine as an example and talks about “surely every medicine is an innovation, and he that will not apply new remedies must expect new evils; for time is the great innovator”(Bacon, of innovation) Bacon describes medicine to patients as knowledge to humans. If people don’t learn new knowledge, their ideas will be antiquated since everything else is developing. When people reject uncertainties, they are rejecting progress and improvements. People who desire certainties will not be able to have innovations. Bacon and Montaigne both argue that desire for certainty restricts people’s knowledge which will eventually keep their ideas antiquated and make people lag behind society.

While it appears that desire for certainty can lead to a life full of denial and ignorance, some might argue that certainty does not cause ignorance since having certainty in God can lead to the discovery of truth which indicates that having certainty may not be a bad thing: “they that deny a God destroy man’s nobility...if he be not kin to God by his spirit, he is a base and ignoble creature”(Bacon, of Atheism). This passage is arguing that having certainty in God makes a man noble and it can help humans in pursuing truth. People having faith in God believes in everything God says or does. People believe that God can bring them truth, but it doesn’t prove that having certainty will lead to truth. Desire certainty in knowledge means rejecting skepticism, but having faith in God does not require people to reject any knowledge. Instead, it actually brings people the courage and confidence to discover new things. God creates humans with the ability to learn and to question as Bacon discussed in of atheism “when he resteth and assured himself upon divine protection and favour, gathereth a force and faith, which human nature in itself could not obtain”(Bacon, of atheism). God provides people the courage and confidence to be skeptical. Having certainty in God does not make people desire certainty for knowledge, instead, they are encouraged to doubt and question knowledge. Therefore desiring certainty in God doesn’t contradict the idea that rejecting uncertainties leads to ignorance. Rather, it supports the idea that was brought up by both Montaigne and Bacon that people should be skeptical. God gives humans the capacity to test and reflect on knowledge, otherwise, they will be ignorant.

As Montaigne and Bacon both agree on, the greatest obstacle in humans’ search for knowledge is the desire for certainty. Humans prefer conventions over innovation. They used to be satisfied with what they know and reject uncertainties. Not being skeptical restricts people’s knowledge. The desire for certainty can lead to a life with denial and ignorance, it lowers people’s awareness for false knowledge and keeps them antiquated. People have a tendency to only listen to what they believe in and reject any contrary ideas without any form of questioning, and it makes people ignorant. People lack self-reflection and judge things based on their own knowledge. They don’t question nor test their own information, which makes it possible for people to learn false knowledge. Also, the desire for certainty means a lack of innovations. People who reject uncertainties are likely to reject innovations which make their ideas antiquated since society is always developing.
